Frequently Asked Questions
Students

How do I join? Do I have to apply?

If you are interested in joining, you can find us at the Student Org Fair. No need to apply, anyone can join! Be sure to visit our booth and sign up for our mailing list: it's also a good opportunity to meet the executives face to face. The latest information about the Org Fair can be found here (we also give away free stickers).

I would like to join but am unable to attend the Student Org Fair. Can I still sign up?

Yep! You can join our mailing list by inputting your information here. Expect to be contacted September 16th with an update about our first meeting! We will not be accepting new members onto the mailing list after September 16th, 2017, so be sure to sign up!

I'm not a Computer Science major, can I still join?

Yes, however our events and curriculum are geared more towards Computer Science / Computer Engineering majors. If you are not a CS major or minor, you are still encouraged to join the club if you have an interest in programming.

What kind of time commitment can I expect in joining the club?

You can expect a weekly time commitment of anywhere from 60 to 90 minutes. Occasionally longer events occur, such as our hackathon. Our mini-hackathon takes place once a semester and lasts 12 hours (10am to 10pm). While members are encouraged to attend, they are not required to. We occasionally have additional events, such as company campus tours, which are optional and last about 1-2 hours.

There's a hackathon?

Yes! For our members only, of course. It's a 12-hour event that happens once a semester filled with contests, prizes, and food! We'll be releasing the full itinerary as the date gets closer; our Fall 2017 Hackathon is currently scheduled for September 23rd at 10am!

Will I have to pay any dues?

Yes, the Software Development Club collects dues to pay for food at events. Though we do have corporate sponsors, we believe our sponsors' money is better spent on resources and events for students. Dues are $10 per member.

What's your attendance policy?

Our policy is not to punish a lack of attendance, but rather to reward those who consistently show up. For each weekly meeting or special event, we have a sign-in sheet. Each time you sign in, you are entering your name into our special attendance raffle. At the end of the semester, we give out three great prizes by drawing names from the raffle pool. The more you attend, the larger chance you have to win! You can see last year's prizes here:

I am interested in joining the executive board, how can I apply?

At the end of every semester we take applications for the board. We consider many factors: your club attendance rate, club engagement, overall demeanor, and technical experience (if you are applying for a position which requires it). We are looking for extremely motivated individuals willing to ensure the long-term success of the SDC, and it doesn't look too bad on your resumé either!
Sponsors & Partners

Where can I get more information about sponsorship?

All sponsorship information can be found in our sponsorship packet.

My organization is unable to contribute monetarily, how can we still get involved with the SDC?

Any organization that comes in to speak to our members is qualified as a partner. Contributions can also be made in hardware or resources (ie. AWS storage, domain names, etc.). We also accept any company apparel donations. Contact one of our outreach representatives if you would like to make a non-monetary contribution.

Who should I contact if I am interested in sponsoring?

New sponsors should contact our president, Ellery Kreloff, at kreloff@wisc.edu. Else, your designated outreach representative will contact you each June about renewing your sponsorship for the next academic year.

I would like to claim the free apparel included in my sponsorship, who should I contact?

Provide your outreach representative with the address you would like the products sent to. Shipping's on us!

Are my organization's contributions tax exempt?

Unfortunately, no. The University of Wisconsin-Madison actively discourages registered student organizations (RSOs) from filing for non-profit
(aka 501(c)(3)) status due to the complicated and lengthy nature of the process. However, be ensured that all RSOs must operate under a "not-for-profit" model, including the SDC.

What kind of presentation should my organization give to the SDC?

The usual presentation formats include information about what it's like to work as a software developer in the "real world" and the history of your organization. In the past we have also hosted: panels of developers and HR representatives taking questions about interview dos an don'ts, tech talks about a new technology being used or launched, and various product demonstrations. Presentations are typically 30 to 45 minutes with time at the end for questions.

Does the SDC have a resumé booklet of its members?

Yes, it is available to Silver and Gold Level sponsors only.
